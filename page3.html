For the Slide

They say to dress for the slide, not the ride. And now I know what they mean. Jeans got split on the right knee and scraped my knee quite badly. My coat was even worse. the sleave had a slit all the way to elbow. It was upon examinig this tear that I realised the coat was not mine. This was Aaron's new under armor coat. I've been borrowing it since he's visiting his parents. It's a $500 coat and I had no clue hoe i WAS TO pay for it, especially since I had probably lost my job at Burger Man(tm). I checked my board, and the reason I fell was that my rear wheel bearings were completely broken. I could not even find the right wheel.

I left my board on the side of the road, and started the walk home. I had given up, I just wanted to go to bed early and start afresh the next day. I took the scenic route home to think about things. As I trugged back, holding the cuff of the tartered coat with my left hand. I caught the smell of earth and fresh grenades. I find myself there almost instinctively and come to in front of a grape stand. I hand the nice lady behind it a fiver and she drops two bunches of them in my open knapsack. I kept a third one in my gloved hand for convenience. I barely make it a block from the stand when I am compeled to eat one of the, to my knowlege, unwashed grapes.

The grape was good
It wasn't so bad
